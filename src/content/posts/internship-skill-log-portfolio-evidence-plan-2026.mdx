A summer internship can disappear into vague resume lines if you wait until the last week to remember what happened. A skill log turns daily work into evidence: what you tried, what improved, what feedback you received, and what can safely become a portfolio example. This June 2026 guide shows students how to document value without oversharing employer, client, school, or coworker information.

![Internship skill log portfolio evidence plan](/images/illustrations/lph-internship-skill-log-20260609-hero.png)

## Evidence decision table

| Work item | Safe log entry | Do not include | Portfolio option |
|---|---|---|---|
| Data cleanup | Tool, scale, quality check | Raw data or names | Synthetic example |
| Meeting notes | Decision, your action | Private strategy | Process reflection |
| Design draft | Skill practiced | Client files without permission | Recreated mockup |
| Customer support | Pattern learned | Personal details | Checklist template |
| Research task | Sources and recommendation | Confidential roadmap | Public-source summary |

![Internship desk with blank notebook](/images/illustrations/lph-internship-skill-log-20260609-01.png)

## Capture the week while it is fresh
At the end of each week, write six lines: main task, skill used, evidence created, feedback received, blocker, and next step. Keep it short enough that you will actually do it. The log is not a diary and not a place to store private documents. It is a memory aid for future interviews, resumes, and learning goals.

## Separate evidence from artifacts
Evidence can be a measurable result, a before-and-after description, a decision you supported, or a skill you practiced. An artifact is a file, screenshot, dataset, deck, code sample, or design. Many artifacts cannot be shared. That does not mean the experience is useless; it means you describe the skill and create a safe public substitute when appropriate.

![Weekly reflection cards with no writing](/images/illustrations/lph-internship-skill-log-20260609-02.png)

## Use a privacy boundary column
Add a column that says public, ask first, internal only, or never share. Client names, personal data, credentials, private dashboards, unreleased products, and coworker feedback usually belong outside a public portfolio. When you are unsure, ask a supervisor with a specific example rather than assuming that a cropped screenshot is safe.

## Turn tasks into resume bullets later
Do not force every weekly note into a polished bullet immediately. First collect accurate facts. Later, convert them into action, method, and outcome: “organized support tags to reduce duplicate triage,” “tested onboarding checklist with five peer reviewers,” or “summarized public competitor research for a team discussion.” Honest specificity beats inflated claims.

![Safe portfolio artifact folders](/images/illustrations/lph-internship-skill-log-20260609-03.png)

## Ask for feedback while you can still use it
A skill log should create better work during the internship, not only after it. Bring one entry to a check-in and ask: What skill should I practice next? What would make this deliverable more useful? Which parts are confidential? This turns reflection into coaching and prevents a final-week scramble for permission.

## AI can organize, not invent
AI tools can help cluster your notes into skills, suggest interview prompts, or check whether a bullet is too vague. They should not invent metrics, expose employer material, or rewrite private content into something that still reveals confidential context. Paste only non-sensitive summaries and verify every claim against your own records.

![Mentor conversation with blank documents](/images/illustrations/lph-internship-skill-log-20260609-04.png)

## End-of-internship package
Before the last day, prepare a private log, a public-safe portfolio list, permission questions, updated resume bullets, and a thank-you note that names specific learning. Save only what policy allows. If access to systems ends, you should still have your own high-level reflections and approved public examples.

![Portfolio review at blank laptop](/images/illustrations/lph-internship-skill-log-20260609-05.png)

## Summary
The best internship log is small, consistent, privacy-aware, and evidence-based. It helps students learn during the role and explain their growth afterward without leaking information.
